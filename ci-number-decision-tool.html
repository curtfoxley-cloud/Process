EIA-649-1A · GEIA-HB-649A · [XXX-CM-PROC-001] · Decision Tool

Same CI or
Separate CIs

For two or more items (different part numbers) that appear related: determine whether they share one CI identifier with part-number variants, or are designated as separate CIs.

Principle — CI Number vs. Part Number

The CI identifier follows the end-use function and the management need for separate configuration control. The Part or Identifying Number (PIN) follows form, fit, and function — it changes whenever a modification creates a non-interchangeable condition. Multiple non-interchangeable part numbers can therefore legitimately live under one CI as variants, and one part number can appear under multiple CIs.

Note — One Part Number, Multiple CIs

The mapping runs both directions. A single part number instantiated in multiple subsystems that perform distinct functions — or whose instantiations fulfill different functional requirements — is assigned a distinct CI per functional instantiation. Example: the same antenna part number installed once in the UHF subsystem and once in the MIDS JTRS subsystem is one PIN but two CIs, because each installation satisfies a different end-use function, sits in a different branch of the CI hierarchy, and needs its own change effectivity address.

Reference: EIA-649-1A §3.2.1 (CI selection), GEIA-HB-649A §7.9 / Appendix D (supplemental selection criteria), §7.4.1 (product identifier issuance and change), and [XXX-CM-PROC-001] §5.3–5.5 and Appendix B (MBSE alignment). Final CI designation authority for Acquirer-controlled CIs rests with the Acquirer. This tool is a decision aid; it does not substitute for engineering judgment or contract requirements.
